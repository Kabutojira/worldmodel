# TSMC Daily Report — 2026-06-21

## What changed

The latest accessible evidence reinforced TSMC's role as the core manufacturing bottleneck in AI. The strongest new signal was not a TSMC filing; it was Taiwan macro data showing AI demand is large enough to move national exports and growth.[^taiwan]

## Thesis impact

- **Base case:** stronger.
- **Bull case:** better supported on scarcity duration.
- **Bear case:** still centered on geopolitical concentration rather than cyclical weakness.

## Main evidence

- Taiwan GDP grew at a **23.6% annualized rate in Q4 2025** and more than **12% YoY**.
- Gross exports exceeded **$63B per month**.
- Manufacturing approached **40%** of the economy as AI-linked chip demand surged.[^taiwan]

## Important connections

- **TSMC -> Nvidia / AI accelerators** remains central.
- **TSMC -> AI data centers** strengthened because every incremental buildout story still collapses into scarce manufacturing capacity.
- **TSMC -> China-Taiwan geopolitical risk** remains inseparable from the bull case, not merely a background footnote.

## Mispricing watch

- The market may still underprice the duration of TSMC scarcity rents while also underpricing the persistence of geopolitical concentration risk.

## Open questions

- How long does packaging scarcity remain as important as wafer scarcity?
- What evidence would show meaningful diversification away from Taiwan?
- Which customers are most exposed if scarcity persists longer than expected?
