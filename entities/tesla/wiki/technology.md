## Latest evidence

- Tesla's Q1 2026 update deck says Cortex 1 is in production at more than 100k H100e-equivalent training compute and Cortex 2 is in early ramp at more than 130k H100e-equivalent, which reinforces Tesla's dependence on Nvidia-class compute and power availability for autonomy training.<a href="https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1318605/000162828026026551/exhibit991.htm">source</a>
- The same deck says paid robotaxi service is ramping unsupervised in Austin, Dallas, and Houston, while San Francisco Bay Area remains in safety-driver mode and Phoenix/Miami are still in preparation. That keeps the thesis in "commercial proof emerging, geographic scale unproven" territory rather than fully validated platform economics.<a href="https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1318605/000162828026026551/exhibit991.htm">source</a>
- Tesla's Q1 2026 production, deliveries, and deployments release reported over 408,000 vehicles produced, over 358,000 delivered, and 8.8 GWh of energy storage deployed. Energy remains large enough to matter, but auto volume still dominates the operating leverage debate.<a href="https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1318605/000162828026022956/exhibit9911111.htm">source</a>
