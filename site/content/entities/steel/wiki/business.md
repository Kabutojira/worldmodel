# Steel Business

## Core model

- Pricing reflects construction/manufacturing cycles, raw material costs, and infrastructure demand.
- Bootstrap focus: price benchmark, supply additions, inventory / processing constraints, and downstream demand.

## Revenue / value drivers

- Primary thesis link: Infrastructure and transformer input.
- Connected-entity mechanisms:
- Automotive market: Automotive market shapes Steel demand, pricing, market share, or capital allocation through adoption, competition, and cycle conditions.
- Wind power: Wind power shapes Steel demand, pricing, market share, or capital allocation through adoption, competition, and cycle conditions.
- Power transformers: Power transformers is a physical bottleneck or enabling network that influences Steel scaling, utilization, and returns.
- Electricity grid: Electricity grid is a physical bottleneck or enabling network that influences Steel scaling, utilization, and returns.

## What to update in daily runs

- Metrics to monitor: steel, automotive steel, electrical steel, transformers.
- Track benchmark prices, project ramps, processing concentration, and substitution trends.
