# AI data centers

## Overview

AI data centers are still the most important physical bottleneck in the current WorldModel. The latest evidence strengthens the case that the constraint is no longer just GPU availability; it is increasingly **power availability, power delivery equipment, and time-to-grid**.

## Current thesis summary

- **Demand remains real:** the power market is reacting as if AI, electrification, and industrial reshoring have created a structurally different load regime.
- **Constraint has moved down-stack:** transformers, interconnection, gas backup, and storage are becoming more decisive than generic "data center demand" headlines.
- **Key implication:** hyperscaler and frontier-model upside continues to transmit into utilities, batteries, transformers, natural gas, and selected semiconductor bottlenecks.[^power-gap][^taiwan]

## Connected entities

- Nvidia: AI buildout still depends on GPU throughput and pricing.
- Amazon / Google / Meta / Microsoft: hyperscaler capex is the main load-growth engine.
- OpenAI / Anthropic / xAI: frontier labs convert model demand into incremental power demand.
- Energy: electricity supply now matters as much as compute supply.
- Electricity grid: interconnection and transmission are direct gating factors.
- Natural gas: often the fastest dispatchable bridge fuel for co-located or backup generation.
- Nuclear power: long-duration solution candidate, but slower to deploy.
- Battery energy storage: increasingly strategic as a flexibility layer, not just a renewables accessory.
- Power transformers: a concrete bottleneck, not an abstract infrastructure note.
